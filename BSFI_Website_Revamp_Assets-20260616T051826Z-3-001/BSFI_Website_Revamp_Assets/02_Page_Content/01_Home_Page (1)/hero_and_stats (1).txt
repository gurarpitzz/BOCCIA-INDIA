Welcome to the Boccia Sports Federation of India

Founded by Jaspreet Singh Dhaliwal, the Boccia Sports Federation of India (BSFI) is dedicated to promoting, developing, and governing the sport of Boccia across the nation. As a Paralympic sport specifically designed for athletes with severe physical disabilities, Boccia provides a platform for skill, strategy, determination, and excellence.

Under the visionary leadership and commitment of its founder, BSFI works tirelessly to create opportunities for athletes at all levels through competitions, coaching and training programs, talent identification initiatives, and awareness campaigns. The federation is committed to building an inclusive sporting environment where athletes can showcase their abilities, pursue excellence, and represent India with pride on national and international stages.

Through our dedication to inclusivity, empowerment, and sporting excellence, we strive to nurture future champions and ensure that every athlete has the opportunity to participate, compete, and achieve their full potential.



The Boccia Sports Federation of India (BSFI) is the apex body for the governance of Boccia Paralympic Sport in India. BSFI is a National Sports Federation (NSF). BSFI is affiliated with the Boccia International Sports Federation (World Boccia), Headquartered in London (UK) https://www.worldboccia.com/about-worldboccia/members/. BSFI working as a National Sports Federation (NSF) in India and an affiliated member of Paralympic Committee of India https://www.paralympicindia.org.in/affiliated-nf-nosd. (Recognised by Govt. of India)

BSFI is a Not-for-Profit organization, working solely to promote Sports among the physically challenged, including the Cerebral Palsy persons in India. It is specially designed for severe disabilities. Boccia has no counterpart in the Olympics. Boccia Sports is one among 22 summer sports played in Paralympic Games beside Asian Para Games.

BSFI was established in India during 2016 and therefore is a new addition to the Paralympic movement as compared to other Para Sports which have existed in India since 1970. The sport of Boccia is therefore in the development stage. Due to the inheritance structure of Boccia Sport and its participating athletes, the expenses involved in competition conduct is fairly high due to engagement of supporting staff, equipment and medical assistance. As compared to other Para sports namely Para athletics, Para Swimming, Para Archery, Para Table Tennis, Para Powerlifting etc.

The athletes participating in Boccia are the most severely disabled and all are wheelchair bound. In view of their highly disabilities, it is imperative to have the support of escorts, Ramp operators and coaches to assist every athlete.

In Boccia Sport, the athlete throws coloured balls (Red or Blue) as close as possible to the target or White ball which is known as Jack. The winner athlete is he/ she who lands the most balls (out of the six balls allocated to each athlete) as close to the jack as possible
